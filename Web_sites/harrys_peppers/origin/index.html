Our Story

We found these peppers in the Loreto district of Peru. Starting in the town of Jaen, we traveled along roads adjacent rivers to multiple small communities. Although each town we visited was very different, we began to notice a trend, something they all had in common: hot sauce. Specifically, a vinegar based sauce made with round, orange peppers that we could not identify. We came to learn that these peppers we called “aji charapitas.” Before leaving the country, we were able to find seeds packaged adequately well enough so that we could take them home. When we returned, we immediately began to draft methods of growing them and reaching out to local restaurants who we thought would be interested in the peppers. Since, they have been incorporated in dishes served at a James Beard Foundation event featuring Peruvian food.
